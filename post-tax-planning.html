ELSS vs NPS: The Smart Investor's Guide to Maximizing Tax Savings

Tax saving dhaan **January** and **March** la periya **headache** ah irukkum. Namma **ELSS (Equity-Linked Savings Scheme)** and **NPS (National Pension System)** maathiri **high-return** products use panni, **tax burden** ah kurakkalaam. Idhudhaan **safe and smart** way da.

The Dual Goals: Tax Saving and Wealth Creation

Unga tax planning, **tax save** panradhu mattum illama, **future wealth** ah kooda create pannanum. ELSS and NPS, rendume market-linked products dhaan. But avanga **goals** and **lock-in period** different.

ELSS (Equity-Linked Savings Scheme): The Growth Option

ELSS dhaan **Mutual Fund** category la **tax save** panna kudukkira option. Idhu **Section 80C** kulla varum (up to ₹1.5 Lakhs).

Key Features of ELSS

  • Lock-in Period: Lowest lock-in period (3 years) dhaan irukkum. Itha time la withdrawal panna mudiyadhu.
  • Tax on Maturity: Withdraw pannum bodhu **LTCG (Long Term Capital Gains) Tax** apply aagum.
  • Portfolio: High equity exposure (mostly 90%+), so **risk** kooda **athigam** dhaan.

NPS (National Pension System): The Retirement/Extra Benefit Option

NPS dhaan **retirement** ku focus pannum. Idhu **extra ₹50,000** deduction ah kudukkum (Section 80CCD(1B)).

The Power of Extra Deduction

Neenga already **80C** ah full ah use pannittenga na, **NPS** la **extra ₹50,000** invest panna, unga total deduction ah **₹2 Lakhs** varaikkum increase pannalaam. Idhu romba periya benefit da.

  • Lock-in Period: **Mandatory lock-in** irukkum (mostly **till age 60**).
  • Portfolio: Asset allocation (Equity, Debt) ah **age-based** panni or **manual ah** choose pannalaam.

Comparative Summary: ELSS vs NPS

Unga goal and time horizon ku yetha maathiri choice pannanum:

Feature ELSS (MF) NPS (Retirement)
Applicable Section 80C (₹1.5 Lakhs) 80C + 80CCD(1B) (Extra ₹50K)
Minimum Lock-in 3 Years Till Age 60 (Very Long Term)
Goal Focus Growth, General Goals Pure Retirement Income
