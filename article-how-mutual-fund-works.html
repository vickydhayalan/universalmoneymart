01

The Complete Money Journey — From Your Bank to Your Returns

When you invest ₹10,000 in a mutual fund, where does that money actually go? What happens behind the scenes? Let's trace the complete journey of your rupee — from the moment you press "Invest" to the moment profits hit your bank account.

1

Step 1 — You Place an Investment Order

You decide to invest ₹10,000 in "Mirae Asset Large Cap Fund." You submit your order through an app, website, or via your MFD (Mutual Fund Distributor like Vignesh Dhayalan). Your bank account is debited the same day or next business day.

Tamil: நீங்க app-ல "Invest" click பண்றீங்க. Bank-ல இருந்து ₹10,000 automatically deduct ஆகும். This is called a "subscription" — நீங்க fund-ல subscribe பண்றீங்க.
2

Step 2 — AMC Processes Your Order at Day's NAV

Your order is processed by the AMC (Asset Management Company). For equity funds, orders placed before 3:00 PM get that day's NAV. Orders after 3:00 PM get the next business day's NAV. If NAV today is ₹100, your ₹10,000 buys 100 units.

Tamil: Cut-off time = 3:00 PM. 3 PM-க்கு முன்னாடி invest பண்ணா → today's NAV கிடைக்கும். 3 PM-க்கு பிறகு → next day's NAV. Formula: ₹10,000 ÷ ₹100 NAV = 100 units உங்களுக்கு.
3

Step 3 — Units Are Credited to Your Folio

Within 1–2 business days, 100 units are credited to your "folio" — your personal mutual fund account number with that AMC. You'll receive a confirmation SMS, email, and can see it in your CAMS/KFintech statement or the investment app.

Tamil: "Folio" = உங்க mutual fund account number. 100 units இப்போ உங்களுக்கு belong ஆகுது. Confirmation SMS/email வரும். CAMS/KFintech-ல பார்க்கலாம்.
4

Step 4 — Fund Manager Invests the Pooled Money

Your ₹10,000 is added to the massive pool of money collected from thousands of other investors. The Fund Manager uses this pool to buy stocks/bonds according to the fund's mandate. Your money becomes part of a diversified portfolio within 1–3 days.

Tamil: உங்க ₹10,000 + ஆயிரக்கணக்கான investors-ஓட பணம் = ₹500 Crore+ pool. Fund Manager இதை 40–70 different companies-ல invest பண்ணுவாரு. Instant diversification!
5

Step 5 — Daily NAV Reflects Your Portfolio Growth

Every evening after market closes (3:30 PM), AMFI calculates and publishes NAV for every mutual fund in India. If the stocks in your fund rose 1% today, NAV rises 1%. Your 100 units × new NAV = your current investment value.

Tamil: Market close ஆனா (3:30 PM), AMFI daily NAV publish பண்றது. Today fund stocks 1% up → NAV ₹100 → ₹101. உங்க 100 units × ₹101 = ₹10,100. ₹100 profit in one day!
6

Step 6 — You Redeem and Money Returns to Bank

When you want to exit, you submit a redemption request. The AMC sells units at that day's NAV. For equity funds, money reaches your bank account within T+3 business days (3 days after the redemption date). Debt funds: T+2 or T+1.

Tamil: 2 years பிறகு NAV = ₹148 ஆனா. நீங்க redeem பண்றீங்க → 100 units × ₹148 = ₹14,800. ₹10,000 → ₹14,800 in 2 years. 3 working days-ல bank-ல வந்துடும்.
🇮🇳 Summary in Tamil

உங்க பணம் → AMC-க்கு போகுது → Fund Manager stocks வாங்குவாரு → Daily NAV change ஆகும் → Profit வந்தா NAV கூடும் → Redeem பண்ணும்போது profit உட bank-ல வரும். உங்க பணம் always AMC-கிட்ட இருக்கும் — distributor-கிட்ட இல்ல. SEBI இதை strictly regulate பண்றது.

02

What is NAV? — A Deep Dive

NAV (Net Asset Value) is the price at which you buy or sell one unit of a mutual fund. It is calculated every business day after market hours and published on the AMFI website.

Think of NAV exactly like a stock price — but instead of one company's share price, it represents the value of the entire portfolio of investments held in the fund.

📖 Real Example — NAV Calculation

Let's Calculate NAV Step by Step

Imagine a mutual fund holds the following investments:

• Reliance Industries shares worth: ₹25 Crore
• Infosys shares worth: ₹20 Crore
• TCS shares worth: ₹18 Crore
• HDFC Bank shares worth: ₹15 Crore
• Cash and other assets: ₹2 Crore
• Total Assets = ₹80 Crore

Management fees payable: ₹0.50 Crore
Other liabilities: ₹0.50 Crore
Total Liabilities = ₹1 Crore

Net Assets = ₹80 Crore − ₹1 Crore = ₹79 Crore
Total units issued to all investors = 79 Lakh units
NAV = ₹79 Crore ÷ 79 Lakh = ₹100 per unit

💡 Next day if Reliance goes up ₹2 Crore and others stay same → Total Assets = ₹82 Crore → NAV = (₹82Cr − ₹1Cr) ÷ 79L = ₹102.53. Your units grew in value!
🇮🇳 NAV — Tamil Explanation

NAV = Fund-ஓட total investment value ÷ Total units. Simple-ஆ சொல்லணும்னா: Fund-ல இருக்கற எல்லா stocks-ஓட market value கூட்டி, outstanding units-ஆல வகுக்கணும் — அதுதான் NAV. Market up → NAV up. Market down → NAV down. AMFI daily 11 PM-க்கு முன்னாடி publish பண்ணும்.

Important: High NAV = Bad, Low NAV = Cheap என்று நினைக்காதீங்க! ஒரு fund ஆரம்பிச்சு 10 years ஆனா NAV ₹500 ஆகலாம் — அது good performance sign! New fund-ஓட NAV ₹10 இருந்தா "cheap" இல்ல — அது just starting point.

💡
Key Insight: NAV is not like a stock price where lower is "cheaper." A fund with NAV ₹500 and one with NAV ₹10 can both be equally good investments — what matters is the fund's portfolio quality, fund manager track record, and expense ratio. Never choose a fund based on NAV alone.

⚠️ Common Mistake: Many beginners avoid funds with high NAV thinking it's "expensive" and prefer new funds with ₹10 NAV thinking they're "cheap." This is completely wrong. NAV history reflects fund performance history — a fund that grew from ₹10 to ₹500 has delivered excellent returns. Choosing a fund purely on low NAV is a costly mistake.

03

Mutual Fund Units — Your Ownership Certificate

When you invest in a mutual fund, you don't receive stocks directly. Instead, you receive units — which represent your proportional ownership in the fund's entire portfolio.

Units work exactly like shares of a company — except instead of owning one company, your units give you proportional ownership in a basket of 30–80 companies.

How You Buy Units

When you invest a lump sum, you receive units calculated by dividing your investment amount by the day's NAV.

The number of units can be in decimals — mutual funds allow up to 3 decimal places. So if NAV is ₹47.50 and you invest ₹5,000, you get exactly 105.263 units.

Formula: Units = Investment Amount ÷ NAV

Example: ₹5,000 ÷ ₹47.50 = 105.263 units
Tamil: ₹5,000 invest பண்ணா, NAV ₹47.50 ஆனா → 105.263 units கிடைக்கும். Decimal-ல units possible — worry பண்ணாதீங்க!

How Your Units Grow in Value

The number of units you hold stays the same (unless you invest more or withdraw). But the VALUE of each unit changes daily as NAV changes.

Your total portfolio value = Number of Units × Current NAV. As the fund's stocks perform well, NAV rises, and your portfolio value increases — even without adding more money.

You hold: 105.263 units
NAV when bought: ₹47.50 → Value = ₹5,000
NAV after 2 years: ₹75.00 → Value = ₹7,894.70

Tamil: Units same — ஆனா NAV ₹47.50 → ₹75 ஆச்சு. உங்க investment ₹5,000 → ₹7,895 ஆச்சு. Units-ஓட count same, value மட்டும் change.

How SIP Adds More Units Every Month

With SIP, every month you invest a fixed amount. Each month's investment buys units at that month's NAV. Since NAV changes monthly, you get different number of units each month — this is called Rupee Cost Averaging.

When NAV is high, your ₹1,000 buys fewer units. When NAV is low (market dip), your ₹1,000 buys more units. Over time, this averages out your cost per unit.

Month 1: NAV ₹100 → ₹1,000 SIP → 10.000 units
Month 2: Market dips, NAV ₹80 → ₹1,000 SIP → 12.500 units
Month 3: NAV ₹90 → ₹1,000 SIP → 11.111 units
Total: ₹3,000 invested → 33.611 units avg cost = ₹89.25/unit

Tamil: Market down-ல more units கிடைக்கும் — இதுதான் SIP-ஓட beauty! Rupee Cost Averaging என்று இதை சொல்வாங்க.

How You Sell (Redeem) Units

When you redeem (withdraw), you specify either the amount you want (e.g., ₹20,000) or the number of units to sell (e.g., 50 units). The AMC calculates the proceeds at that day's NAV.

You can do partial redemptions — sell some units and keep the rest growing. There's no obligation to sell all units at once.

You hold: 105.263 units, Current NAV: ₹95
Partial redemption: Sell 50 units → ₹50 × 95 = ₹4,750 to bank
Remaining: 55.263 units still invested, keep growing

Tamil: Full-ஆ withdraw பண்ண வேண்டாம். Partial-ஆ withdraw பண்ணலாம். Rest continue to grow. 3 working days-ல money bank-ல வரும்.

Who is the Fund Manager? — The Person Managing Your Money

The Fund Manager is a highly qualified investment professional employed by the AMC to manage the fund's portfolio. When you invest in a mutual fund, this person makes all the buying and selling decisions on your behalf.

Think of the Fund Manager as a chef who decides what ingredients to buy, in what proportions, and when to change the recipe — all to make the best meal (returns) for the guests (investors).

🎓
Qualifications Required
Must be NISM (National Institute of Securities Markets) certified. Typically CA, CFA, MBA Finance, or equivalent. Years of market research experience mandatory.
🔍
What They Do Daily
Research companies, analyse balance sheets, meet management teams, attend analyst calls, study macroeconomic data, and decide which stocks to buy, hold, or sell.
⚖️
Bound by Fund Mandate
Cannot invest outside the fund's stated objective. A Large Cap fund manager must buy large cap stocks — cannot invest in small caps even if they look attractive.
👥
Team Support
Never works alone. Supported by a team of research analysts, economists, risk managers, and compliance officers. It's an institution managing your money — not one person.
📊
Performance Accountability
Compared against a benchmark index (e.g., Nifty 50). Must consistently outperform. If a fund manager consistently underperforms, the AMC replaces them.
💰
How They're Paid
Paid by the AMC from the expense ratio (annual management fee charged on AUM). You don't pay the fund manager directly — it's already factored into NAV.
📖 A Day in the Life of a Fund Manager

What Does a Fund Manager Actually Do?

6:00 AM: Reviews overnight global market movements — US markets, Europe, Asian futures. Checks if any macro event affects Indian markets today.

8:00 AM: Team meeting with research analysts. Reviews quarterly earnings reports of 15 companies in the portfolio. One company missed earnings — decision: hold or sell?

10:00 AM: Market opens. Executes buy/sell orders through the dealing desk. Buys 5 lakh shares of a company at ₹450 because research shows it's undervalued.

2:00 PM: Conference call with the CFO of a company they're considering adding to the portfolio. Asks about growth plans, debt levels, and competitive landscape.

4:00 PM: Market closes. Reviews day's portfolio performance vs benchmark. Prepares monthly fact sheet and attribution analysis.

💡 Tamil: Fund Manager full-time உங்க பணத்தை manage பண்றாரு. நீங்க daily market பார்க்க வேண்டாம் — இதுதான் MF-ஓட biggest advantage!

"A good fund manager is not someone who never makes wrong calls — it's someone who makes more right calls than wrong ones, and manages risk intelligently when wrong."

— Vignesh Dhayalan, AMFI Registered MFD (ARN-288927)
🇮🇳 Fund Manager — Tamil Summary

Fund Manager = உங்களுக்காக invest பண்ற expert. NISM certified, years of experience, research team support. Daily stocks research பண்றாரு, buy/sell decisions பண்றாரு. உங்க expense ratio-ல இருந்தே salary பெறுவாரு — உங்களுக்கு separate charge இல்ல. Fund Manager change ஆனா (AMC switch பண்ணும்போது) — அது ஒரு risk factor ஆகும். Good fund manager = consistent track record பாருங்க.

07

How Returns Are Generated in Mutual Funds

Mutual fund returns come from 3 sources. Understanding each one helps you choose the right fund for your goal.

📈
Capital Appreciation
When stocks in the fund rise in price, the fund's NAV rises. This is the primary source of returns for equity funds. Long-term, equity NAV grows reflecting company earnings growth.
💵
Dividends from Stocks
Companies in the fund pay dividends to shareholders. The fund receives these dividends, which either get reinvested (in Growth plan) or distributed to you (in IDCW plan).
🏦
Interest Income
For debt funds, returns come primarily from interest on bonds and government securities. More predictable than equity, lower risk, lower returns.
📖 Example — How ₹1 Lakh Grows in 10 Years

The Compounding Power of Mutual Fund Returns

You invest ₹1,00,000 (₹1 Lakh) one-time in an equity mutual fund. The fund's NAV grows at an average of 12% per year (rough historical average for diversified equity funds).

Year 1: ₹1,00,000 × 1.12 = ₹1,12,000
Year 2: ₹1,12,000 × 1.12 = ₹1,25,440
Year 3: ₹1,25,440 × 1.12 = ₹1,40,493
Year 5: ₹1,76,234
Year 7: ₹2,21,068
Year 10: ₹3,10,585

₹1 Lakh → ₹3.10 Lakh in 10 years. Without doing anything. Just staying invested. Compare this to FD at 6.5%: ₹1 Lakh → ₹1.87 Lakh in 10 years.

💡 Tamil: ₹1 Lakh invest பண்ணி 10 years wait பண்ணா → ₹3.10 Lakh. FD-ல same money → ₹1.87 Lakh மட்டுமே. Difference = ₹1.23 Lakh! Long-term-ல compounding இப்படித்தான் work பண்ணும்.

Growth Plan vs IDCW (Dividend) Plan — Which to Choose?

Every mutual fund offers two variants: Growth Plan and IDCW Plan (Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal — formerly called Dividend Plan). Choosing the right one affects how your returns work.

FeatureGrowth PlanIDCW Plan
What happens to profits?Reinvested automatically — NAV keeps growingDistributed as periodic payouts to your bank
NAV behaviourKeeps rising over timeFalls after each distribution (profit removed)
Compounding effectMaximum — profits generate more profitsReduced — profits taken out regularly
Tax on payoutsOnly when you redeem (LTCG/STCG)Taxed as income in the year received
Best forLong-term wealth buildingRetirees needing regular income
Recommended for beginners?Yes — always choose GrowthOnly if you need regular income
✅
Simple Rule: If you're investing for long-term wealth creation (retirement, child education, etc.) — always choose Growth Plan. IDCW is only for retirees or people who need periodic income from their investment. For all others, Growth Plan's compounding effect builds far more wealth over time.
🇮🇳 Growth vs IDCW — Tamil

Growth Plan: Profit reinvest ஆகுது → NAV keep growing → Compounding maximum → Long-term wealth best → Tax only when sell பண்றீங்க.

IDCW Plan: Profit periodically distribute பண்றாங்க → NAV down ஆகும் → Compounding less → Tax that year income-ஆ count ஆகும்.

Verdict: Beginner, young investor, long-term goal — Growth Plan choose பண்ணுங்க. Retirement income வேணும்னா மட்டும் IDCW.

How Redemption Works — Getting Your Money Back

Redemption is the process of selling your mutual fund units and getting money back in your bank account. It's simpler than most people think — and usually takes just 1–3 business days.

1

Submit Redemption Request

Log in to your app/AMC website, go to your folio, select the fund, and click "Redeem." Choose either the amount (₹20,000) or number of units (50 units) you want to sell.

Tamil: App-ல போய் "Redeem" click பண்ணுங்க. Amount (₹20,000 withdraw) அல்லது Units (50 units sell) — ஏதாவது ஒன்னு specify பண்ணுங்க.
2

Cut-off Time Determines NAV

Equity funds: request before 3:00 PM → same day NAV applied. Request after 3:00 PM → next day NAV. Debt funds: cut-off is also 3:00 PM but settlement is faster (T+1 or T+2).

Tamil: 3 PM cut-off important! 2:30 PM-ல request பண்ணா → today's NAV. 4 PM-ல request பண்ணா → tomorrow's NAV. Urgent need-ஆ இருந்தா 3 PM-க்கு முன்னாடி submit பண்ணுங்க.
3

Units Are Debited from Folio

The corresponding units are immediately marked for redemption and removed from your folio. You'll receive a redemption confirmation via email and SMS.

Tamil: Folio-ல இருந்து units remove ஆகும். Email/SMS confirmation வரும். இந்த step-ல உங்களுக்கு ஒன்னும் பண்ண வேண்டாம் — automatic ஆகும்.
4

Money Hits Your Bank Account

For equity funds: T+3 business days (3 working days after redemption date). For debt funds: T+1 or T+2. Money goes directly to your registered bank account — the same one used for SIP investment.

Tamil: Equity fund → 3 working days. Debt fund → 1–2 working days. Money directly registered bank account-ல வரும். ATM withdraw பண்ணலாம், UPI use பண்ணலாம் — normal bank money போல.

⚠️ Exit Load Alert: Some funds charge an "Exit Load" if you redeem within a certain period (typically 1 year for equity funds, at 1%). This is deducted from your redemption proceeds. Always check the fund's exit load before investing, especially for short-term investments. ELSS funds have a mandatory 3-year lock-in and cannot be redeemed before that.

🇮🇳 Exit Load — Tamil

Exit Load = Early withdrawal penalty. Most equity funds — 1 year-க்கு முன்னாடி withdraw பண்ணா 1% charge பண்ணுவாங்க. 1 year-க்கு பிறகு withdraw பண்ணா → Exit Load = 0 (free). ELSS = 3 years lock-in, ஒரே ஒரு exception. Invest பண்றதுக்கு முன்னாடி fund-ஓட exit load scheme document-ல check பண்ணுங்க.

For open-ended mutual funds (the most common type), NAV = the price at which you buy or sell units. It's calculated once per day after market close. You always transact at this NAV — no premium or discount.

Market price is different from NAV only for Closed-Ended Funds and ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds) which trade on stock exchanges like shares — their market price can be above (premium) or below (discount) their actual NAV. For regular open-ended mutual funds, you always get exactly the NAV value.
