The Unbreakable Foundation: Why Your Emergency Fund Must Be Built First

Financial Planning la **Emergency Fund** dhaan first and most crucial step. Neraiya peru adhai **ignore** panni, **direct ah investing** ku poiduvanga. Adhu romba **periya risk** da.

What is an Emergency Fund and How Big Should It Be?

Emergency Fund na, **unexpected expenses** (e.g., medical emergency, sudden job loss, car repair) varum bodhu, unga **investments** ah touch pannaama irukka thevaiyana cash reserve dhaan.

  • Minimum Size: **6 months** oda unga **fixed monthly expenses** (EMI, Rent, Bills).
  • Target Size: **9 to 12 months** oda expenses (especially self-employed and single-income families).

The Cost of NOT Having a Fund (Why it Destroys SIPs)

Emergency Fund illa na, **financial risk** varum bodhu, neenga **kandippa** unga **SIPs** (Mutual Funds) illa **long-term FDs** ah break panna vendiyadhu irukkum. Adhu dhaan **compounding benefit** ah azhikkum.

Risk: Market low ah irukkum bodhu, neenga **forced ah** unga investments ah **sell** panna vendiyadhu irukkum. Adhu **permanent loss** ah create pannum.

Where to Keep Your Safety Net? (Liquidity vs. Returns)

Emergency Fund oda goal **Return** illa da. **Liquidity** (easy ah edukkum capacity) and **Safety** dhaan mukkiyam. Idho, **moondru best options**:

Prioritizing Layer 1: The Final Word

Insurance (Term and Health) and Investing (SIPs) start panradhukku munnadi, **Emergency Fund** ah **fully fund** pannunga. Idhudhaan unga **financial future** ah protect pannum.
