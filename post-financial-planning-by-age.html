Financial Planning for Different Ages: Asset Allocation for Your 20s, 30s, and 40s

Unga **vayasuku yetha maathiri** dhaan **risk capacity** kooda **change** aagum. Young ah irukkum bodhu **high risk** edukkalaam. **Retirement** pakkathula varum bodhu, **safety** ku poga vendum. Idhudhaan **Life-Stage Financial Planning** da.

The Core Principle: Time is Your Biggest Asset

Time dhaan **compounding** ah **magic** ah panna help pannum. Unga **time horizon** romba athigam irundha, **market volatility** ku **risk** kammiya irukkum. Adhunaala, time dhaan **aggressive equity allocation** ku **permission** kudukkum.

Phase 1: The 20s (Aggressive Growth)

Focus: Maximum Equity Exposure

Goals: Emergency Fund build panradhu (Layer 1) and **Retirement** ku start panradhu.

  • Allocation: 80% Equity (SIP) / 20% Debt (Emergency Fund).
  • Product Choice: Small Cap, Mid Cap and Flexi-Cap Funds.

Phase 2: The 30s (Balancing Growth and Responsibility)

Focus: Increasing Protection and Goals

Goals: Child's Education/Marriage, Home Loan, increasing life cover.

  • Allocation: 65% Equity / 35% Debt.
  • Strategy: Increase **Term Insurance** cover and use **Step-Up SIP** to meet rising goals.

Phase 3: The 40s (Consolidation and De-risking)

Focus: Capital Preservation and Retirement Check

Goals: Retirement Corpus check pannanum. High risk ah avoid pannanum.

  • Allocation: 50% Equity / 50% Debt.
  • Strategy: **Small Cap** funds ah avoid pannanum. **Large Cap** and **Hybrid Funds** la focus pannanum.

Final Word: The 100 Minus Age Rule

Oru **simple rule** irukku: **(100 - Your Age)**. Adhu dhaan unga **maximum Equity allocation** irukkanum. Example: 30 years old na, 70% Equity varaikkum edukkalam. Idhu **rough guide** dhaan, aana **risk management** ku nalladhu.
