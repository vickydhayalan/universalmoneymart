Section 01 — Basics

What is a Mutual Fund?

A Mutual Fund is a pool of money collected from many investors and invested in stocks, bonds, or other securities by a professional fund manager.

Tamil Explanation: Mutual Fund என்பது ஒரு பொது நிதி திட்டம். பல investors-ஓட பணத்தை ஒன்னா சேர்த்து, ஒரு professional fund manager அதை stocks, bonds-ல invest பண்ணுவாரு. உங்களுக்கு market knowledge இல்லாட்டாலும் invest பண்ணலாம் — இதுதான் முக்கிய advantage.
🏦

Many investors contribute. Even ₹500/month from each person creates a large fund collectively.
👨‍💼
Fund Manager
A SEBI-registered professional manages your money using research and expertise.
📊
Diversification
Your money is spread across many stocks/bonds — so one company's loss doesn't hurt much.
💰
Start with ₹500
No need for lakhs. Start a SIP with just ₹500/month and build wealth over time.

Section 02 — Understanding

How Does a Mutual Fund Work?

When you invest in a mutual fund, you buy units at the current NAV (Net Asset Value). As the underlying investments grow, the NAV increases — and so does your wealth.

NAV என்றால் என்ன? NAV = Net Asset Value. ஒரு mutual fund unit-ஓட price. Example: ஒரு fund-ஓட NAV ₹50 இருந்தா, ₹5000 invest பண்ணா உங்களுக்கு 100 units கிடைக்கும். Fund-ஓட value கூடுனா NAV கூடும் → உங்க investment-உம் கூடும்.
  • 1

    You invest money

    You put ₹5,000 into a mutual fund. You receive units based on the current NAV.

  • 2

    Fund manager invests

    The fund manager pools your money with other investors and buys a diversified portfolio of stocks/bonds.

  • 3

    NAV changes daily

    The NAV is recalculated every day based on market performance. If stocks rise, NAV rises.

  • 4

    You earn returns

    When you redeem (sell) your units, you receive the current NAV × number of units = your profit.

Section 03 — Types

Types of Mutual Funds

There are 3 main categories. Each suits a different goal and risk level.

எந்த type உங்களுக்கு சரி? Long-term wealth build பண்ண → Equity. Safe-ஆ money park பண்ண → Debt. Balance வேணும் → Hybrid. இதை பத்தி மேலும் தெரிஞ்சுக்க, full article படிங்க.
📈
Equity Funds
Invest in stocks. High risk, high return potential. Best for 5+ year goals. Sub-types: Large Cap, Mid Cap, Small Cap, ELSS.
🏛️
Debt Funds
Invest in bonds & fixed income. Lower risk, stable returns. Good for short-term goals (1–3 years).
⚖️
Hybrid Funds
Mix of equity + debt. Balanced risk. Good for moderate investors who want growth with some stability.

Section 04 — SIP

What is SIP? (Systematic Investment Plan)

SIP is a method of investing a fixed amount in a mutual fund at regular intervals (monthly/weekly). It's NOT a product — it's a way to invest.

SIP = Monthly automatic investment. ஒவ்வொரு மாசமும் ₹500, ₹1000, ₹5000 — எந்த amount-உம் automatically invest ஆகும். Market up-ஆ இருந்தாலும், down-ஆ இருந்தாலும் invest continue ஆகும். இதனால Rupee Cost Averaging benefit கிடைக்கும்.

Section 06 — Safety

SEBI & AMFI — Is Your Money Safe?

Mutual Funds in India are regulated by SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India) and the industry body is AMFI (Association of Mutual Funds in India). Your money is NOT with the distributor — it's directly with the AMC.

🏛️
SEBI
Regulates all mutual funds. Every AMC, fund manager, and distributor must be SEBI registered. Protects investor interests.
🤝
AMFI
Industry body. All MF distributors must have ARN number from AMFI. Vignesh Dhayalan ARN: 288927.
🔒
Your money is safe
Money goes directly to the AMC (SBI MF, HDFC MF, etc.) — NOT to the distributor. Distributor only earns commission from AMC.

Section 09 — Risk

Understanding Mutual Fund Risk

Mutual funds are subject to market risk — but risk can be managed through diversification, time horizon, and fund selection.

Risk = Bad இல்ல! Risk இல்லாம return இல்ல. ஆனா risk-ஐ understand பண்ணி manage பண்ணலாம். Long-term-ல invest பண்ணா risk naturally குறையும். SEBI ஒவ்வொரு fund-க்கும் Risk-o-Meter கொடுக்குது — அதை பாருங்க.

SEBI's Risk-o-Meter — 6 risk levels:

Low Low to Moderate Moderate Moderately High High Very High
📉
Market Risk
Stock prices fluctuate. NAV goes up and down. Long-term holding reduces this risk significantly.
🏢
Concentration Risk
If a fund holds too many stocks of one sector, one sector crash hurts more. Diversified funds reduce this.
💧
Liquidity Risk
Most mutual funds can be redeemed within 1–3 working days. ELSS has 3-year lock-in.
